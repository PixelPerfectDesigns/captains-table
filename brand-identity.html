Overview

Who We Are

The Captain's Table has been a cornerstone of Monroe, NY since 1934. We are a family restaurant with deep community roots — known for great food, cold drinks, and live music on the Afterdeck every Friday and Saturday night. Our brand reflects that heritage: warm, confident, and unpretentious.

Brand Personality
Warm & Welcoming
Confident & Direct
Rooted in Tradition
Lively & Inviting
Unpretentious
Brand Promise

"Great food. Cold drinks. Live music."

Every element of our brand should reinforce this promise — from the warmth of our photography to the confidence of our typography.

Visual Identity

Logo Usage

The wordmark is our primary logo. Use it consistently and never alter its proportions, colors, or spacing.

On light / cream backgrounds
On navy / dark backgrounds
Clear Space & Rules
Always
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "T" in "Table" on all sides
Use on white, cream, navy, or dark backgrounds only
Scale proportionally — never stretch or compress
Never
Place on busy photographic backgrounds without a scrim
Recolor, rotate, or add effects to the wordmark
Use a minimum size below 120px wide in digital contexts

Brand Voice

Voice & Tone

We speak plainly. We're a 90-year-old family restaurant, not a startup. Our voice is warm and direct — like a recommendation from someone who's been coming here for years.

We Sound Like
Bold and simple — "Great food. Cold drinks. Live music."
Confident without being boastful
Warm and familiar — "When you're here, you're family"
Grounded in heritage — "Est. 1934 · Monroe, NY"
Short sentences. Active voice.
We Don't Sound Like
Overly formal or corporate
Trendy or chasing buzzwords
Vague — "an experience like no other"
Apologetic or hedging
Emoji-heavy or overly casual
Copy Examples
Hero Headline
"Great food.
Cold drinks.
Live music."
CTA Button
"Reserve a Table"
(not "Book Now" or "Click Here")
Section Intro
"Live bands on the Afterdeck every Friday and Saturday night, all season long."
Heritage Line
"We've been feeding the community since 1934."

Visual Identity

Photography Style

Our photography is warm, real, and lived-in. Avoid sterile food photography with white backgrounds. We prefer images that feel like they were taken on a good night at the Table.

Exterior · Context & Location
Atmosphere · People & Moments
Venue · Spaces & Ambiance
Dining · Food & Experience
Treatment Rules
Always apply a gradient overlay when text appears over images
Hero images use the navy-blue bottom gradient: dark → navy
Warm, golden-hour lighting preferred
Avoid
Bright white studio food shots
Stock photography of generic restaurants
Heavy filters or over-saturated edits
